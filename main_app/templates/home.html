Space Needle
Seattle, USA

The Space Needle is the most recognizable landmark in the Pacific Northwest. Built in 1962, the Space Needle served as the focal point of that year's World's Fair, during which it hosted 2.3 million visitors. Located on the Seattle Center grounds, the Space Needle was the tallest building west of the Mississippi River at the time it was built.

Big Ben
London, England

Big Ben is the nickname for the Great Bell of the striking clock at the north end of the Palace of Westminster in London and is usually extended to refer to both the clock and the clock tower. The official name of the tower in which Big Ben is located was renamed Elizabeth Tower in 2012.

Sydney Opera House
Sydney, Australia

The Sydney Opera House is a multi-venue performing arts centre at Sydney Harbour in Sydney, New South Wales, Australia. The building comprises multiple performance venues, which together host well over 1,500 performances annually, attended by more than 1.2 million people.
